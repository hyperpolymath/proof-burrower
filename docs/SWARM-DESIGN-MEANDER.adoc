= Burrower Swarm: Design Meander
:toc: left
:toc-title: Wandering through
:sectnums:

A meandering design exploration of Burrower-as-swarm. Written during
the live build session 2026-04-26 — iterating on the user's challenge:
*does this idea make sense, and what is its great form?*

Updated as the design wanders. Not a spec. A thinking surface.

---

== Does the swarm idea make sense?

Short answer: **yes, profoundly — and for non-obvious reasons.**

=== Why "yes" is non-obvious

It's tempting to dismiss the GCHQ-style swarm as "just N rankers in
parallel." If that's all it is, it's not interesting — N parallel
weak rankers gives a weakly-noisy ensemble, modest gains.

That dismissal misses three structural facts:

1. **Mathematical knowledge is genuinely siloed.** A topologist and an
   algebraist read the same goal differently. "Open" means open-set
   to a topologist, open-game to a game-semanticist, open-system to a
   physicist. Token-based ranking treats them as identical. A
   specialist encodes context that disambiguates.

2. **It mirrors how mathematicians actually work.** When a new theorem
   arrives at a math department, the right specialist is found and
   asked. Burrower-as-swarm is that intuition externalised — and
   automated, available 24/7 on every commit.

3. **It composes with proof tactics.** Once a goal is routed to (say)
   order theory, the relevant tactics (lattice fixpoints, well-founded
   induction) are different from algebra tactics (group actions,
   structure theorems). The HOME informs the PROOF STRATEGY. A swarm
   that locates well prepares ECHIDNA to prove well.

=== Where the analogy holds and where it stretches

GCHQ has a Russia desk, a China desk, a signals desk. They share intel
through structured products (assessments, briefings, indications and
warnings). Each desk has its own analytic tradecraft.

Mathematics has algebra, topology, logic, combinatorics. Specialists
have their own tradecraft: tactics that work in their domain, lemma
naming conventions, foundational assumptions, preferred libraries.

The analogy holds for: domain expertise, structured intel sharing,
desk-vs-desk collaboration on boundary objects.

The analogy stretches at: GCHQ desks compete for resources and may
withhold intel; math swarms must SHARE freely. GCHQ has secrecy
tradecraft; math swarms must be radically transparent — every
specialist's reasoning trace must be readable by every other.

== Trial-and-error multiplication

User-supplied refinement (2026-04-26): _"scalable swarms seems to me
to give additional trial and error opportunities to us on top of that
[transformations], so long as we can find a way to get them to
orchestrate and share in a useful way their approaches."_

This reframes the swarm from "parallel rankers" to **parallel
explorers of the proof-strategy space**. Each specialist tries its
domain-specific heuristic. Wins propagate. Losses inform.

Concretely, the multiplication looks like:

[cols="1,3,3"]
|===
|Specialist |What it tries |What it shares back

|Algebraist
|"This is a semiring monotonicity lemma. Try `add_mono` after
 instantiating to a class instance."
|"Approach failed: `tropical` is not in `ordered_ab_semigroup_add`.
 Workaround: use `trop_add_le_iff` directly. Recorded as pattern
 `tropical-not-ordered-class`."

|OrderTheorist
|"This is monotonicity in a complete lattice. Try `mono_setI` +
 `complete_lattice_class.SUP_mono`."
|"Approach succeeded for the *finite* case via `Sup_finite_mono`.
 Recorded as pattern `finite-set-sup-monotonicity`."

|Combinatorialist
|"Walks of length k form a finite, decomposable family. Try
 `sum.subset_diff` after unfolding `walks_def`."
|"Worked. Recorded as pattern `walk-sum-decomposition`."
|===

The multiplication isn't 3x — it's exponential in the breadth of
patterns the swarm collectively learns. Every recorded pattern
becomes available to the next agent on the next goal.

== The shared knowledge layer

The user's critical insight: trial-and-error only multiplies if agents
**share approaches, methods, and results in a useful way**.

Without sharing, N agents are N independent guessers. With sharing,
N agents become a research lab.

=== Layer name candidates

* **The Burrow Ledger** — append-only record of every approach tried,
  by which agent, on which goal, with what outcome.
* **The Commonplace Book** — historical metaphor: a shared notebook
  of patterns, citations, and observations.
* **The Blackboard** — classical AI architecture term (Hayes-Roth).
  Less evocative but precise.

Provisional choice: **Burrow Ledger** for the append-only record;
**Pattern Library** for the curated, queryable distillation.

=== Record structure (provisional)

Each ledger entry is a `LedgerRecord`:

[source,toml]
----
[record]
id = "uuid-v7"  # time-orderable
goal_hash = "sha256:..."
goal_text = "lemma trop_walks_sum_mono_subset: ..."
specialist = "Algebraist"
timestamp = "2026-04-26T14:23:11Z"

[approach]
description = "Apply add_mono after class instance"
tactics_attempted = ["add_mono", "ordered_semigroup_class.add_mono"]
preconditions_assumed = ["tropical :: ordered_ab_semigroup_add"]

[result]
status = "failed"  # | "succeeded" | "partial" | "abandoned"
explanation = "tropical type does not implement required class"
artifacts = []  # paths to partial proofs, counterexamples, etc.

[learning]
pattern_extracted = "tropical-not-ordered-class"
pattern_kind = "anti-pattern"  # what NOT to try
generalisation = "Tropical algebra needs explicit hierarchy class instance"
visible_to = ["Algebraist", "OrderTheorist", "Combinatorialist"]
----

=== Pattern Library

Distilled from ledger entries that recur. A pattern is a
`(precondition, approach, outcome)` tuple validated across multiple
goals. Patterns have:

* **Confidence** — count of confirming records.
* **Refutations** — count of records where the pattern was tried and
  failed (informs the precondition).
* **Citers** — which specialists rely on it.
* **Anti-pattern flag** — patterns marked "always avoid" carry
  negative knowledge equally important to positive.

The Pattern Library is queryable: a new agent on a new goal can ask
"what patterns matching my domain + this goal-shape have worked
recently?" before trying anything.

== Orchestration

The orchestration question is: **how do agents coordinate without
becoming a centralised bottleneck?**

=== Three orchestration modes

==== Round-robin (today's MVP)

Each specialist gets the goal in turn (or in parallel). Each returns
a Reading. Synthesis layer combines. Simple. Works.

Limitation: no agent learns from the others within a single goal.

==== Iterative (next)

The first round produces Readings. The second round, each agent sees
the *other agents' Readings* and may revise. A topologist seeing the
algebraist say "this is a semiring fact" may withdraw or sharpen its
own reading.

This is the negotiation phase. It surfaces real disagreements. Records
those disagreements as boundary-object signals.

==== Pipeline (later)

Specialists are organised into a pipeline: PhilosopherOfMath reads
first ("this is constructive / classical / HoTT-style"), then domain
specialists ("inside that foundation, this is algebraic"), then
proof-strategy agents ("inside that domain, try these tactics").

Pipeline composition is more disciplined but requires a clear DAG of
specialist dependencies.

=== Coordination protocol (sketch)

Specialists communicate through structured messages on the Burrow
Ledger. The protocol has four verbs:

* `propose <approach>` — "I think we should try X"
* `report <result>` — "I tried X and got Y"
* `cite <prior>` — "this is similar to ledger entry #1234"
* `withdraw <id>` — "my earlier proposal #789 is wrong, here's why"

A coordinator (the head agent) reads the protocol stream and issues
synthesis verdicts. The coordinator never forces agents to agree —
disagreement is informative.

== Build-on / learn-from

The user's third requirement: agents must produce work that **others
can build on or learn from**.

This makes the ledger entries *load-bearing* — not just diagnostic.
Specifically:

=== Build-on

A successful approach is a **partial proof artifact**. A specialist
that closes a sub-goal records the proof script it used. The next
agent on a similar sub-goal can:

1. Find the prior partial proof via Pattern Library lookup.
2. Adapt the script (e.g. via the synonym table from the
   `echidna suggest` plan in `AI-WORK-echidna.md` §3).
3. Submit the adapted script. Record outcome.

=== Learn-from

A failed approach is a **negative witness** — it tells future agents
NOT to waste time on this path. Negative knowledge is as valuable as
positive: a search space of size N with M known-bad regions is
effectively a search space of size N-M.

Equally: a partially-failed approach (it worked for `nat` but not
`tropical`) is a **specialisation signal** — the pattern's
preconditions need refinement.

=== The reading layer

Beyond raw records, the ledger supports a **reading layer** —
human-readable summaries that other agents (or humans) can consume:

* "Recent finding: Burrower's Algebraist consistently fails on
  tropical-typed goals because tropical lacks ordered_ab_semigroup_add.
  Workaround: explicit `trop_add_le_iff` reasoning. (cited 3x in
  the last 24h.)"

The reading layer is what makes the swarm a **learning organisation**
rather than a memoryless ensemble.

== What scales with the swarm

The user's framing — "scalable swarms" — invites the question: what
exactly scales?

* **Coverage scales linearly** with the number of specialists. Each
  added specialist covers one more domain.
* **Trial-and-error breadth scales linearly** — N specialists each
  trying their preferred approach is N attempts per goal.
* **Pattern library size scales sub-linearly** — patterns are
  deduplicated; new agents tend to rediscover existing ones at first.
* **Orchestration cost scales quadratically** — N agents emitting M
  messages each gives O(N\*M) reads. This is the bottleneck. Mitigate
  via topic-filtered subscription (an Algebraist subscribes only to
  algebra-tagged messages by default).
* **Foundational divergence costs scale unpredictably** — when
  PhilosopherOfMath says "this is constructive" but the Algebraist
  uses classical tactics, you get noise. Mitigate by attaching
  foundation tags to every pattern.

The right size for the swarm is probably 10–20 specialists at first,
with hierarchical sub-swarms (e.g. "the algebra desk" containing
GroupTheorist, RingTheorist, LinearAlgebraist as sub-specialists)
emerging as needed.

== Where Burrower meets ECHIDNA's failure modes

From `AI-WORK-echidna.md` §1, ECHIDNA's failure modes that the swarm
naturally addresses:

[cols="1,3"]
|===
|ECHIDNA failure |Swarm response

|Wrong abstraction
|The right specialist proposes the right abstraction. The synthesis
 layer flags multi-specialist boundary objects where abstraction
 choice matters most.

|Synonym blindness
|Each specialist carries its own synonym table for its domain.
 Cross-domain synonyms surface in the ledger.

|Wrong induction principle
|The Combinatorialist knows finite-set inductions; the OrderTheorist
 knows well-founded inductions; the right one is invoked.

|Cross-domain analogy
|This is Burrower's *strength*. A goal that an Algebraist sees as
 algebra and a Topologist sees as topology is exactly the kind of
 cross-domain analogy that ECHIDNA can't see by itself.

|Goal generalisation
|Each specialist proposes the generalisation native to its domain.
 The user picks.

|Combinatorial blow-up
|The ledger records "blow-up zones" so future agents avoid them.
|===

Burrower-the-swarm is genuinely **complementary** to ECHIDNA-the-
prover, not a competitor. ECHIDNA dispatches finished proofs;
Burrower locates the goal and proposes strategy *before* the prover
runs. Together they are more than the sum.

== Where this becomes a great form

A great form of this system would have:

* **Specialists definable in 007** — agents that make agents.
  Adding a new domain means writing a 007 file, not patching Rust.
* **A shared Burrow Ledger** persisted in VeriSimDB (per estate
  policy: `feedback_verisimdb_policy.md`) so the knowledge survives
  restarts and can be queried historically.
* **A Pattern Library** that surfaces useful generalisations weekly
  via a digest agent ("This week's emergent patterns: …").
* **An adversarial sub-swarm** — a Devil's Advocate specialist that
  *deliberately* proposes counter-readings, forcing the others to
  defend their claims with stronger evidence. (Inspired by
  `feedback_full_battery_before_claims.md`.)
* **ECHIDNA integration** as a native ledger consumer: when ECHIDNA
  fails to close a goal, it posts to the ledger, swarm specialists
  read, and the most-engaged one proposes a remediation.
* **Per-specialist confidence calibration** — each specialist learns
  how often it's been wrong on which kinds of goals; high-confidence
  agents are weighted more in synthesis. This is Bullshit-Field
  reasoning per `project_bullshit_field_design.md` applied to the
  swarm itself.
* **Compile to typed-wasm** so the whole swarm is portable: run
  inside the editor, in CI, in the browser, on a serverless edge.
* **Hidden first** — refine the shape in private; release when the
  pattern library has matured enough to be useful out of the box.

== Open questions

. How do we keep the ledger from becoming a junk drawer? (Probably:
  expiration policy + relevance decay + periodic distillation by a
  curator agent.)
. How do specialists handle disagreements with each other? (Negotiate
  via the protocol; surface persistent disagreements as boundary
  objects; never auto-resolve.)
. How do new specialists bootstrap their domain expertise? (Bootstrap
  from existing patterns + a hand-written keyword set + the corpus
  from their domain's seed library.)
. What's the right granularity for a "specialist"? (Probably one per
  AMS-MSC top-level subject, with sub-specialists for active areas.)
. How do we audit the swarm? (Every recommendation traces to ledger
  entries. Reproducibility means archived ledgers per release.)

== Provisional verdict

The idea is sound and has more reach than I initially saw. The
critical addition from this meander: **the swarm without a shared
knowledge layer is just an ensemble; the swarm WITH a shared ledger
is a learning organisation that genuinely scales trial-and-error
into compounding mathematical knowledge.**

Burrower's MVP today (corpus index + 3 specialists + synthesis) is
the substrate. The ledger and pattern library are the next two
levers. 007 is the language for adding agents at scale. ECHIDNA
becomes a downstream consumer.

The form is becoming great.
